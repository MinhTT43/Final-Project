    What is "visually impaired"?

    Visual impairment, also known as vision impairment or vision loss, is a decreased ability to see to a degree that causes problems in daily activities such as driving, reading, socializing, and walking. The most common causes of visual impairment globally are:


      Uncorrected refractive errors such as being svere near- or far-sightedness.

      Cataracts a disease where sight becomes cloudy.

      Glaucoma is the deterioration of the optical nerves.

    Let us look at an example.

    Imagine being on your way to the bus stop and you see your ride coming ahead of schedule. Knowing this is the last bus that takes off for the evening you hurry up trying to catch the bus. Being visually impaired you sharpen your senses to the best of your ability but those are also affected by your lack of vision:


      Sight: Having trouble seeing things from afar, you can either focus on the blur overview or focus on the details that are important.

      Touch: Having poor sight means you have to rely on your other senses to navigate around. You are given a cane to assist you occupying one hand. In your other you are holding your phone with an app open telling you where to go and what to look out for.

      Hearing: You are equipped with hearing devices that are given feedback from the app. With these plugged in your ears, you are told what to expect in front of you.

    With all your senses occupied you have a considerably higher risk of getting into an accident. Ambient sounds from the environment are blocked, sight is a struggle and touch is interfered by having to handle multiple tasks. Having your main perspective sense taken away is challenging.
