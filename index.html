    The challenge

    What can we do to support individuals who are separated from society because of their visual impairment?

    What is "visual impairment"?

    The definition of visual impairment is “a decrease in the ability to see to a certain degree that causes problems not fixable by usual means, such as glasses.” (Wikipedia 2020, WHO 2020). Globally, the most common causes of visual impairment are:


      Uncorrected refractive errors such as being severe near- or far-sightedness (Wikipedia 2020, WHO 2020).

      Cataracts a disease where sight becomes cloudy (Wikipedia 2020).

      Glaucoma is the deterioration of the optical nerves (Clinic, M. 2020).

    How is it to be visually impaired?

    Visual impairments pose many challenges (Langelaan, Boer et al. 2007, WHO 2019):


    • Significantly more time spent performing simple tasks.
    • Being able to respond to sudden changes in the environment.
    • Having to rely on multiple devices to be able to coordinate independently.

    Being visually impaired is no easy task. In addition to these difficulties, there are many underlying ones.

    Did you know?

    Globally there are more than 1 billion people that are moderate to severely visually impaired. Even with glasses, most can not effectively carry out everyday tasks even with multiple assisting devices (WHO 2020).

    Physical challenges

    The most difficult task for someone who has recently lost their vision, is to adapt and adjust to their new lifestyle. Some activities become too difficult to perform, and others require significant effort to re-learn (Langelaan, Boer et al. 2007, envision 2019).

    Exercise

    Physical activities are becoming substantially more difficult.

    Cooking

    Cooking requires more attention as labels and objects are harder to recognize.

    Discomfort

    More prone to pain and discomfort from not being as physically active.

    Navigation

    Increased difficulty navigating through unfamiliar environments.

    Mental health

    People who experience reduction in vision or other visual impairments are at much greater risk of experiencing mental illnesses. 45 percent of those with mild to severe visual impairment show signs of depression when adapting to their new life (Langelaan, Boer et al. 2007, envision 2019).

    Social life

    Difficulties retaining a social lifestyle and maintaining friendships.

    Learning

    Education and learning in general becomes more difficult due to lack of resources fit for the visually impaired.

    Partners

    Insecurities and low self-esteem impacts one’s ability to seek out new relationships, both romantically and platonically.

    Depression

    There is an increased likelihood for anxiety and depression if one suffers from visual impairment.

    The technology

    Horizon provides an Enhanced Vision platform for AR devices, with the aim to increase mobility for the visually impaired. We utilize deep learning to provide accurate Object Tracking technology. Visual impairment can happen to anyone, but we can work together to create a better and more inclusive future for everyone.
