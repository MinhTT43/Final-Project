    The challenge

    What can we do to support individuals who are distanced from society because of their visual impairment?

    What is "visually impaired"?

    The definition of visual impairment is “a decrease in the ability to see to a certain degree that causes problems not fixable by usual means, such as glasses.” Globally, the most common causes of visual impairment are:


      Uncorrected refractive errors such as being svere near- or far-sightedness Clinic, M. (2020).

      Cataracts a disease where sight becomes cloudy Wikipedia (2020).

      Glaucoma is the deterioration of the optical nerves Wikipedia (2020).

    How is it to be visually impaired?

    Being visually impaired introduces a lot of inconvenience. Some of this may sound trivial, but they pose to be real challenges for many. Not being able to identify people by their facial features are reportedly one of the most difficult changes to cope with. Along with this here is a list of other complications.


    • Spend significantly more time to perform a simple task.
    • Being able to respond to sudden changes in the environment.
    • Having to rely on multiple devices to be able to coordinate independently.

    As you can probably figure, being visually impaired is no easy task. In addition to these apparent difficulties, there are many underlying ones.

    Did you know?

    Globally there are more than 1 billion people that are moderate to severely visually impaired. Even with spectacles, most cant effectively carry out every day tasks even with multiple assisting devices WHO (2020).

    Physcial challenges

    The most difficult task for someone that had their vision suddenly changed, is to adapt and adjust to their new lifestyle. Some physical activities are becoming too difficult to maintain, and some require a lot of effort to re-learn . (Langelaan, Boer et al. 2007, envision 2019).

    Execsize

    Physical activities are becoming substantially more difficult.

    Cooking

    Cooking requires much more attention as labels and objects are harder to recognize.

    Discomfort

    You are much more prone to pain and discomfort for not being as physically active.

    Navigation

    Withoutgood sight, navigating around puts much more strain on your ability to hear, touch and feel.

    Mental health

    Even though their homes are a safe space, being isolated and alone puts a lot of pressure on one's mental health. People who have their sight taken away have a much greater risk of experiencing depression and anxiety. The study indicates that 45 per cent of those with mild to extreme visual impairment have signs of depression when adapting to their new life (Langelaan, Boer et al. 2007, envision 2019).

    Social

    Difficulties maintaining the strong bond you had with your friend circle.

    Learning

    Education and learning new things becomes incredibly challenging.

    Partners

    Insecurities and low selfesteem impacts on how often you seek out relationships.

    Depression

    Increased likelihood of anxiety and depression.
