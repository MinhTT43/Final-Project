    THE HORIZON

    The challenge

    Globally there are at least 1 billion people that have near or distance vision impariment [3], affecting their ability to perform activites related to self-care or handle everyday life situations [1].

    What does it mean?

    Vision impairment can be defined as vision loss caused by low vision or blindness. And is not fixable by usual means, such as the use of glasses or contacts [2]. Challenges that visual impaired faces may vary, while it can vary from not being able to identifying foodlabels and other types of labes to the problems they might face while navigte through unkown areas without tactile paths [3][4]. Living with this disability will therefore have a substantial negative impact on the quality of life [5].

    Why is it important?

    Good health and well-being is UN's 3rd sustainable development goal, and is the one who inspired us to develop this technology. Reason being, that the main problems of being visually impaired revolves around daily tasks, mobility, depression and anxiety [5]. Our goal is to make it easier to perform dailytasks, such as reading labels or busstables, and easier to travle from A to B by enhancing their vision and highlight the enviorment. Overall increasing their feeling of independency and reduce depression and anxiety.
