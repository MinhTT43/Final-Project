    Did you know ?

    Globally there are more than 1 billion people that are moderate to severely visually impaired. Even with spectacles, most cant effectively carry out every day tasks even with multiple assisting devices.

    What does it mean to be visually impaired?

    Being visually impaired means to be unable to see or have an incredibily difficult time interpreting and trying to understand what is present infront of you. In addition to having trouble with the visual aspect of the disability, both physical challenges and mental disorders are often linked to the disability.

    Let us look at an example.

    Imagine being on your way to the bus stop and you see your ride coming ahead of schedule. Knowing this is the last bus that takes off for the evening you hurry up trying to catch the bus. Being visually impaired you sharpen your other senes to the best of your ability but you also have to consider these factors:


  • Having poor sight you have to navigate forward carefully witha cane on your main hand, bumping into surfaceses.
  • In your off hand you are holding your phone with an app that tells you where you're headed, requiring you to both look and press a variety of buttons.
  • In addition you are wearing hearing devices that provides feedback from the app on your phone, dampening the ambienient sound from the surrounding environment.

    • With all your senes occupied you have a considerably higher risk of getting into an accident. Therefore you let the buss pass and opt for waiting for the next possible ride.
