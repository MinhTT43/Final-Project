    The Challenge

    Globally there are more than 1 billion people that have near or distance vision impairment [1], affecting their ability to perform activites such as self-care or tackle everyday life situations

    What does it mean? - The definition of visual impairmen

    Vision impairment is defined as vision loss caused by low vision or blindness which cannot be corrected by usual means, such as use of glasses or contact lenses [2].

    Challenges imposed on those with visual impairment vary widely from not being capable of identifying objects to complete blindness. Common challenges for most are accomplishing simple everyday tasks and difficulties when navigating around in the environment [3] [4].

    Living with such a disability will therefore have a substantial negative impact on the quality of life [5].

    The UN's 3rd development goal “Good health and well being” has been our main inspiration in developing this groundbreaking technology.

    People who suffer from visual impairment struggles to perform everyday tasks such as reading labels, distinguishing objects and navigating around. By not being able to perform these simple independent tasks, we can see a clear correlation between the disability and the development of additional physical and mental disabilities (Har vi kilde?).

    Our goal is to make it easier for those who are shackled by this impairment so they can function more independently and strengthen their quality of life and improve their well being.
